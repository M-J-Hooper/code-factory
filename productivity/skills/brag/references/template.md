# Brag Document Template

Use this template when creating new monthly documents at `~/log/YYYY-MM/brag.md`.

## Template

```markdown
# Brag Document — YYYY-MM

## Direct Impact

Projects, features shipped, technical decisions, and measurable outcomes.
This is the core of your engineering contribution.
Include: what you built, decisions you drove, quantified impact (saved X dollars, improved Y by Z%).

## Building / Team Formation

Team creation, hiring processes, onboarding new members, establishing team rituals.
Include: job descriptions written, team-building interviews, onboarding plans, working agreements.

## Mentorship

### Current

<!-- Active mentoring relationships. Format: * Name (start date - ongoing, context) -->

### Past

<!-- Completed mentoring relationships. Format: * Name (period, context) -->

## Interviewing

| Type | Count |
|------|-------|

<!-- Track by interview type: System Design, Team Match, Coding, etc. -->

## Engineering Culture

### Operational Practices

Bar-raising activities: incident response, postmortem reviews, rollout standards,
process improvements, book clubs, internal tooling.

### Docs & Talks

Documents written, presentations given, internal blog posts, knowledge-sharing sessions.
Format: * [Title](URL) (context: event, audience, date)

### Book Clubs & Learning Groups

Organized or participated in learning activities.

## Guilds & Groups

Community participation: ERGs, interest groups, cross-org initiatives.
Format: * Group Name — your role or contribution

## Consulting

Cross-team expertise sharing, subject-matter-expert work, advisory roles.
Include: who asked, what topic, what outcome, who was involved.

## Customer Engagement

### Cooperative Work

Embeds, co-KRs, paired work with other teams.
Format: * Team (duration, period) — what you did, what you learned

### Discovery

User research, needs assessment, stakeholder interviews, use-case mapping.

### Syncs & CABs

Regular cross-team meetings and advisory boards.
Format: * Meeting Name — cadence, your role

## Learning

Skills acquired, courses completed, books read, new tools mastered, certifications.
Focus on: what you learned and how it makes you more effective.

## Goals

### Current Objectives

What are you working toward this quarter/half?

### Progress

How are you tracking against those objectives?

## Uncategorized

Items that need manual sorting into the sections above.
```

## Section Guidance

Each section maps to a career dimension.
When writing entries, focus on impact and context — not just what you did, but why it mattered.

| Section | Career Dimension | What Reviewers Look For |
|---------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Direct Impact | Technical execution | Shipped work, sound decisions, measurable outcomes |
| Building / Team Formation | Organizational growth | Hiring, team setup, culture definition |
| Mentorship | People development | Developing others, multiplier effect on the team |
| Interviewing | Company building | Pipeline contribution, interview quality |
| Engineering Culture | Technical leadership | Standards, practices, bar-raising across the org |
| Guilds & Groups | Cultural contribution | Community involvement, inclusion, belonging |
| Consulting | Force multiplication | Cross-team impact, expertise sharing |
| Customer Engagement | Product awareness | User empathy, discovery, embedded work |
| Learning | Growth mindset | Continuous improvement, new skills applied |
| Goals | Strategic alignment | Working toward team/org objectives |

## Writing Tips

- **Quantify impact** where possible: "reduced flaky test rate by 40%" beats "fixed flaky tests"
- **Include context**: explain why the work matters, not just what you did
- **Link everything**: Google Docs, Confluence pages, PRs, Jira tickets
- **Capture fuzzy work**: code review quality, onboarding help, unplanned consulting
- **Don't undersell**: if you drove a decision, say "drove" not "helped with"
- **Record as you go**: details fade fast — capturing weekly beats a marathon session
